Krishna's 20 Lessons: Your Life Will Change After This

1. Perform Your Duty Without Attachment

Lesson: Focus on your duty (dharma) without attachment to the results.
Context: Krishna teaches Arjuna that performing one's duty is essential, and one should not be attached to the fruits of their actions. This is highlighted in Bhagavad Gita 2.47: "You have the right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions."

2. Selfless Service (Seva)

Lesson: Engage in selfless service to others without expecting anything in return.
Context: Krishna explains the importance of karma yoga, the yoga of action, which emphasizes performing duties for the welfare of others. Prabhupada often emphasized serving humanity as a service to God.

3. Control Your Mind and Senses

Lesson: Mastery over one’s mind and senses leads to spiritual advancement.
Context: In Bhagavad Gita 6.6, Krishna states that the mind is the friend of the conditioned soul, and when controlled, it can be the best friend; otherwise, it becomes the worst enemy. Prabhupada elaborates that controlling desires is essential for achieving peace.

4. Embrace Change and Impermanence

Lesson: Understand that everything is temporary; attachment leads to suffering.
Context: Krishna emphasizes in Bhagavad Gita 2.14 that the material world is full of dualities, and one must learn to endure them. Prabhupada teaches that recognizing the impermanence of life helps in detachment.

5. Equanimity in Success and Failure

Lesson: Maintain balance in success and failure; both are part of life.
Context: Krishna advises Arjuna in Bhagavad Gita 2.48 to remain steadfast and treat success and failure equally. Prabhupada explains that such equanimity is essential for inner peace.

6. The Importance of Faith

Lesson: Faith is a fundamental aspect of spiritual progress.
Context: In Bhagavad Gita 17.3, Krishna speaks of the three kinds of faith corresponding to the three modes of material nature (sattva, rajas, and tamas). Prabhupada emphasizes that one's faith determines their spiritual trajectory.

7. Surrender to God

Lesson: Complete surrender to God brings liberation.
Context: In Bhagavad Gita 18.66, Krishna instructs Arjuna to surrender to Him completely. Prabhupada highlights that this surrender leads to divine protection and guidance.

8. Seek Knowledge and Wisdom

Lesson: Knowledge is the foundation for making right decisions.
Context: Krishna imparts wisdom through jnana yoga (the yoga of knowledge). Prabhupada stresses the importance of seeking spiritual knowledge for self-realization.

9. Live a Life of Compassion

Lesson: Compassion towards all living beings is essential.
Context: Krishna teaches that all living entities are His parts and parcels. In Bhagavad Gita 14.5, the compassion towards all beings is rooted in understanding our connection with God.

10. Practice Patience and Perseverance

Lesson: Patience and perseverance are crucial in the face of challenges.
Context: Krishna encourages Arjuna to be patient and steadfast. Prabhupada notes that determination is key to overcoming obstacles.

11. Detachment from Material Possessions

Lesson: Detachment from material possessions leads to spiritual freedom.
Context: In Bhagavad Gita 5.10, Krishna explains that one who performs their duty without attachment is not affected by sin, much like the lotus flower untouched by water. Prabhupada reinforces this idea in his teachings.

12. Understand the Eternal Self (Atman)

Lesson: Recognizing your eternal nature leads to liberation.
Context: Krishna emphasizes the immortality of the soul in Bhagavad Gita 2.20. Prabhupada elucidates that realizing our true nature helps transcend material suffering.

13. The Role of Devotion (Bhakti)

Lesson: Devotion to God is the highest path to spiritual realization.
Context: In Bhagavad Gita 12.6-7, Krishna elaborates on the significance of devotion. Prabhupada emphasizes that bhakti is the most direct path to God.

14. The Dangers of Ego

Lesson: Ego leads to separation from God.
Context: Krishna cautions against the false ego in Bhagavad Gita 13.31. Prabhupada emphasizes that ego leads to a sense of individuality that distances us from God.

15. Unity in Diversity

Lesson: Appreciate the diversity of life while understanding unity.
Context: Krishna teaches that He resides in all beings. Prabhupada explains that recognizing this unity helps in fostering harmony.

16. Pursue Your Dharma

Lesson: Following your dharma is paramount for a fulfilling life.
Context: Krishna urges Arjuna to fulfill his kshatriya dharma in Bhagavad Gita 2.31. Prabhupada notes that adherence to one’s duty contributes to overall harmony in society.

17. Avoiding Greed

Lesson: Greed leads to misery; contentment brings peace.
Context: In Bhagavad Gita 3.16, Krishna explains the destructive nature of desire. Prabhupada often stated that moderation and contentment are key virtues.

18. Importance of Gratitude

Lesson: Cultivating gratitude opens the heart.
Context: Krishna teaches the importance of recognizing the gifts of life. Prabhupada emphasized that gratitude is a vital aspect of devotion.

19. The Power of Forgiveness

Lesson: Forgiveness frees the heart and soul.
Context: Krishna emphasizes in Bhagavad Gita 16.3 that forgiveness is a divine quality. Prabhupada notes that holding grudges leads to suffering.

20. Embrace Love and Peace

Lesson: Love and peace are the ultimate goals of life.
Context: Krishna's life epitomizes love and peace. Prabhupada teaches that by loving God and serving others, one finds true peace.
