The Lifting of Govardhan Hill

Govardhan Leela is a famous pastime of Lord Krishna, narrated in Srimad Bhagavatam, where He lifts the Govardhan Hill to protect the residents of Vrindavan. Here's a brief summary according to ISKCON teachings: The people of Vrindavan used to worship Lord Indra, the god of rain, every year, offering grand sacrifices to please him for rainfall. However, Lord Krishna, who was only seven years old at the time, questioned the necessity of this worship. He suggested that they should instead worship Govardhan Hill, the cows, and the brahmanas, who are all essential for the sustenance of their lives. Following Krishna's advice, the residents of Vrindavan performed a grand festival to honor Govardhan Hill. Lord Indra became furious upon seeing this and, feeling insulted, sent torrential rains and thunderstorms to punish them. The rains were so severe that they threatened to flood Vrindavan. To protect the villagers, Lord Krishna lifted the entire Govardhan Hill with His little finger, using it as an umbrella for seven days and nights, sheltering the people and animals from the storm. Amazed by Krishna's divine power, the residents of Vrindavan took shelter under the hill with full faith in Him. Eventually, realizing his mistake, Indra stopped the rains and sought forgiveness from Lord Krishna. Krishna forgave him, and this pastime demonstrated His supreme position and His love for His devotees. This event also highlights the importance of devotion to Krishna over the worship of demigods for material benefits. The Govardhan Puja festival is celebrated each year to commemorate this pastime, emphasizing the worship of Krishna and the simple living in harmony with nature.
